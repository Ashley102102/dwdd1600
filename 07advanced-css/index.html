Rodeo History

How Rodeo Began!

The first recorded rodeo event took place in Deer Trail, Colorado, in 1864. It involved a group of cowboys competing against each other in activities showcasing their ranching skills, such as horse breaking and cattle roping. This event laid the foundation for the rodeo competitions we know today.

What is rodeo?

Rodeo is a competitive equestrian sport that originated from the working practices of cattle herding in Spain and Mexico. It involves riding, roping, wrestling steers, and riding broncs and bulls with events timed to determine winners

Events

Events involves horses and other livestock, designed to test the skill and speed of the cowboys and cowgirls. There are 8 different events they compete in, they are:

  1. Bareback Riding
  2. Steer Wrestleing
  3. Teamroping
  4. Saddle Bronc Riding
  5. Tiedown Roping
  6. Breakaway Roping
  7. Barrel Racing
  8. Bull Riding

Events in Rodeos:

Saddle Bronc Riding
Horse and rider are in chute, once the contestant nods their head the chute opens. Contestant must ride a bucking horse for 8 seconds, the contestant holds on to the horse with one hand using a six-foot braided rein that is attached to the halter of the horse. Two judges score the ride, 100 points possiable 50 for the horse 50 for the rider, 25 for each judge to give the rider and the horse. Highest score wins.
Tiedown Roping
Contestant is in a box while the calf is in a chute. The contestant nods their head and the chute opens and the calf is realeased. The calf gets a head start by a rope called a barrier, once the barrier breaks the time starts. Contestant must rope the calf off the back of a horse, stop and dismout the horse, run down the rope, flank and tie the calf with a piggin string, including a wrap and a hooey and a flag is dropped to stop the time. Fastest time wins.
Barrel Racing
Contestant is riding a horse and runs around barrels in a cloverleaf pattern. Time starts when they cross the timerline and the time stops when the cross going back through. Fastest time wins.
Breakaway Roping
Contestant is in a box while the calf is in a chute. The contestant nods their head and the chute opens and the calf is realeased. The calf gets a head start by a barrier, once the barrier breaks the time starts. Contestant must rope a calf off the back of a horse and stop the horse the rope, that was tied using a small breakable string, breaks off the saddle horn and the time stop. Fastest time wins.
